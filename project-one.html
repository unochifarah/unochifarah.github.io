Case Study 01

AWS SaaS Sales Analysis

Tools Excel · Tableau Public
Dataset 9,993 Transactions
Coverage 48 Countries · 3 Regions
Period 2020 – 2023
01 — Context

Revenue Was Growing. Profit Wasn't.

A global AWS SaaS company was generating $2.3M in B2B transactions across 48 countries — but aggregate revenue was masking a structural problem. 18.7% of all orders were loss-making, and entire region-segment combinations were operating at negative margins without anyone noticing.

The business had no systematic view of where margin was being destroyed — and no framework to understand how discount rates were driving those losses. This project was built to answer that.

Business questions answered:

  • — Which region generates the most revenue vs. the most profit?
  • — Is there a discount level where profitability consistently collapses?
  • — Which customer segment contributes the most revenue vs. profit?
  • — Which product and region combos should the sales team prioritize?
Key Finding

18.7% Loss Rate

1,871 loss-making orders out of 9,993 — plus 65 break-even orders. Total unprofitable transactions: 1,936 (19.4% of all orders).

Root Cause

Discount Abuse

Orders with 65%+ discounts result in –50% profit margin. Even a 20% discount drops margin from 30% to 9%. No guardrails existed.

02 — Approach

How I Framed the Analysis

Two parallel workstreams — one in Excel for cleaning and pivot analysis, one in Tableau for visual storytelling — designed to answer the same questions from different angles.

Step 01

Clean the Foundation

Power Query pipeline to resolve locale-based decimal formatting issues (Indonesian system locale misreading separators), type casting, and feature engineering before any analysis began.

Step 02

Engineer the Metrics

Created calculated columns that didn't exist in the raw data: Profit Margin %, Discount Band (None/Low/Medium/High), Profit Flag, and Revenue Tier — the backbone of every downstream analysis.

Step 03

Decompose by Dimension

5 pivot tables segmented by Region × Year, Product, Segment, Discount Impact, and Industry — each designed to answer one specific business question rather than act as a general summary.

Step 04

Tell the Story Visually

A 5-part Tableau Story built with a narrative arc — from macro overview down to specific product and geographic recommendations — with captions designed for a non-technical audience.

03 — Process
01 / Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering

Building a trusted foundation in Power Query

Ingested 9,993 raw transaction records. Identified and resolved a locale-based decimal formatting issue — the Indonesian system locale was reading . as a thousand separator, corrupting numeric fields on import. Applied M transformations for conditional columns, custom columns, and locale-based type casting before any analysis began.

Power Query M Transformations Type Casting
02 / Excel Pivot Analysis & KPI Dashboard

5 pivot tables, one interactive dashboard

Built five pivot tables covering: Revenue by Region & Year, Product Performance, Segment Analysis, Discount Impact, and Industry Breakdown. Each pivot fed into a slicer-connected KPI dashboard with Region and Segment filters. KPI boxes used named cell references to stay live as slicers changed. Custom number formatting ($#,##0.0,"M") and sheet protection were applied for a clean, shareable output.

Pivot Tables Slicers Named References Excel
03 / Tableau Story — 5-Part Visual Narrative

From macro overview to specific recommendation

Assembled 5 visualizations into a Tableau Story with narrative captions. Charts used: filled world map (geographic margin view), product bar chart, discount vs. profit scatter plot, stacked area chart (segment trends over time), and industry heatmap. Calculated fields — Profit Margin %, Discount Band, Profit Flag, Revenue Tier — were rebuilt in Tableau using IF/ELSEIF logic to mirror the Excel model.

Tableau Public Calculated Fields Scatter / Heatmap Filled Maps
04 / Recommendation Synthesis

Turning findings into an actionable policy

The analysis concluded with two concrete recommendations: cap discounts at 30% company-wide to prevent margin collapse, and investigate the SMB pricing strategy in APJ specifically — where the segment runs at –2.5% overall margin despite being the company's highest-revenue segment globally.

05 — Key Findings
Revenue vs. Profitability

EMEA leads in revenue — but AMER has the best margin.

  • —Overall profit margin is 12.5%, within normal SaaS range
  • —SMB drives 50% of revenue, but APJ SMB runs at –2.5% margin
  • —Q4 (Sep–Dec) spikes consistently across all segments; Apr–Aug is stagnant
  • —Russia: large market, –0.2% margin vs Austria at 30%
Discount Impact

Above 40% discount — losses are almost guaranteed.

  • —65%+ discounts → –50% profit margin
  • —20% discount already drops margin from 30% to 9%
  • —Best combo: Miscellaneous in EMEA at 30% margin
  • —Worst combo: Tech in APJ at –11.4% margin
  • —Top product by revenue: ContactMatcher — but not all top-5 are profitable

07 — Reflection
Lesson 01

Aggregates hide the problem

The 12.5% overall margin looked healthy until I broke it down by region and segment. APJ SMB at –2.5% only becomes visible when you stop looking at totals and start looking at intersections.

Lesson 02

Data quality is the first deliverable

The locale-based decimal issue would have gone unnoticed and silently corrupted every downstream calculation. Fixing it before analysis was the most important step in the whole project.

Lesson 03

Two tools, one story

Excel and Tableau each answered the same questions differently — one for exploration and filtering, one for narrative. Keeping calculated metrics consistent between them was critical to maintaining a single source of truth.

Lesson 04

Findings only matter with a recommendation

Knowing Tech in APJ has a –11.4% margin is an observation. Recommending a 30% discount cap and an SMB pricing audit in APJ is actionable. The analysis was only complete once it concluded with something the business could act on.
